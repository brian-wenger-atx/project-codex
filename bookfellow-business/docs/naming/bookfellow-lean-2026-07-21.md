## IP risk / TM — eyes open (not blocking pre-alpha)

**Melbourne app:** [bookfellow.app](https://bookfellow.app/) — same word, same category (reading companion + AI chat), already in private beta. They cite *The Bookfellow* (1899 AU literary magazine); that history does **not** block us, but they’ve claimed the modern reading-companion lane under this name.

### TLD vs trademark (plain language)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Does `.io` vs `.app` **clear** trademark conflict? | **No.** TM offices and courts care about the **word** “Bookfellow” + what you **sell**, not the suffix. |
| Can a **different TLD screw you across borders**? | **Indirectly, yes — not because TM “follows TLD.”** (1) **Discovery:** users search “Bookfellow app” — App Store / Google don’t separate `.io` from `.app`. (2) **Their rights follow *use* and *registration country*, not your domain:** AU prior use or an AU TM can still matter if you sell globally online. (3) **Your TLD doesn’t grant rights elsewhere:** owning `bookfellow.io` doesn’t give you AU or US trademark rights by itself. (4) **Practical win:** different TLD avoids *domain* squatting and keeps channels distinct until you’re public — it does **not** replace clearance. |
| US-first GTM help? | **Some.** Friends alpha + Reddit beta skew US/low visibility → lower chance of collision *early*. Risk rises at App Store, paid ads, publisher “Official Bookfellow” co-marketing. |

### “Classes 9 and 42” — what that means

Trademarks are filed in **Nice classes** — buckets of goods/services. Examiners ask: would consumers think these two marks in **these buckets** come from the same company?

| Class | Plain English | Why it matters for us |
|-------|---------------|------------------------|
| **9** | Downloadable software, mobile apps, “computer programs for…” | A reading-companion **app** lives here. |
| **42** | SaaS, “non-downloadable software,” design/dev of software | Web product, cloud companion, API-backed chat. |
| **41** (sometimes) | Education, entertainment, publishing services | If positioned as “literary companion” / book-adjacent service. |

Melbourne Bookfellow + our product = same **words** in overlapping **9 / 42** (and maybe 41) → that’s the conflict shape, regardless of `.io` vs `.app`.

### Cross-border C&D — US (you) vs AU (Melbourne them)

*Not legal advice — pattern summary for planning; 1–2 hr TM counsel before App Store if worried.*

**What a C&D is:** A lawyer letter **demanding** you stop — not a court order. You can ignore it; they must **file suit** (or use **platform complaints**) to force anything. Most small-app conflicts stop at letter + App Store, not full international trial.

| They can demand (typical letter) | Can they **enforce** on a US operator? |
|----------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Stop using “Bookfellow” in **Australia** | **Strongest** if they have AU TM or clear AU prior use in same class |
| Stop using the name **in the US** | **Weaker** unless they have **US** TM registration or meaningful **US** use before you |
| Stop using **bookfellow.io** globally | **Overreach** in letter unless they win a court or **UDRP** — different TLD + your legitimate use is a defense |
| Pay **their** profits / your profits / legal fees | Only after **judgment** or settlement — not from C&D alone |
| **Transfer** your domain | Almost never without court/UDRP win |

**Where they’d actually fight**

| Venue | Likelihood | Notes |
|-------|------------|-------|
| **Apple / Google trademark complaint** | **Highest practical risk** | No court needed; Apple may ask you to rename, delay listing, or coexist. Often hits before any lawsuit. **SEO ranking doesn’t matter here.** |
| **AU court** (Trade Marks Act / passing off) | Medium if you sell to AU users | They sue in Australia; you’d defend or settle. Enforcing AU **money** judgment in US is **possible but slow/expensive** for them. |
| **US federal court** (Lanham Act) | Lower **unless** they have US TM or US trade | They need a US hook: registered US mark, or their US commerce/confusion story. US-first alpha helps **your** side but isn’t immunity. |
| **UDRP** (domain grab) | Low for **bookfellow.io** | You own a different domain, use it in good faith for same product — hard case for them vs cybersquatting. |

**What “territorial TM” means for you:** AU rights ≠ automatic US rights. US rights ≠ automatic AU rights. **Who used first in which market** and **who registered where** drives outcomes. Racing them to **US Intent-to-Use** + documented US launch (friends alpha, Reddit) builds **your** US priority — doesn’t erase their AU position.

**Brian lean (2026-07-21):** Outperform on Reddit/App Store/web is a **GTM** goal — it can **increase** visibility and trigger platform or legal contact **faster**, not slower. Legal worry is separate from promotion.

**If a C&D arrives:** (1) don’t panic — read who sent it and which **country/registration** they cite; (2) **don’t reply** with admissions before counsel; (3) check USPTO/IP Australia for their marks; (4) compare to **Red** triggers → pivot ladder if blocking US TM or App Store already acting; (5) one consult often cheaper than a wrong reply.

### US trademark plan (Brian — file before App Store)

*Not legal advice — execution checklist.*

#### Can a foreigner get a US trademark?

**Yes.** Any person or entity can apply to the USPTO. **Foreign-domiciled** applicants must use a **US-licensed trademark attorney** (USPTO rule). **Brian (US)** can file **pro se** or with counsel — attorney still recommended for identical-mark risk.

#### Filed vs registered — do you need to wait to go live?

**No — don’t wait for registration** (often **8–14+ months** after filing).

| Stage | What you have | OK to go live? |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Nothing filed | Domain + intended use only | **Web friends alpha / private invite** — lowest risk |
| **ITU filed** (pending) | Priority date + serial number | **Yes** — placeholder, Reddit beta, US web; cite pending mark in App Store materials |
| **Registered** (®) | Full US registration | Strongest for disputes — still not global |

**Lean:** Go live on **web** now. **File ITU before real launch / App Store** — not required for placeholder. Registration is the long pole; **filing** is the gate before store.
