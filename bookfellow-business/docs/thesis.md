# Product thesis (locked lean — 2026-07-19)

## Job

**Companion for your books** — while-reading / while-listening recall and engagement — **not** a book substitute.

## Why people drop books

They often don’t go back because they forgot where they were, what they already read/heard, or any usable detail — sometimes even what they just listened to. The product exists to:

1. **Solidify immediately after** a chapter or listening session — lock it in while fresh.
2. **Jog memory before returning** — re-entry feels like continuing, not starting over.

Audio ~2‑min jog + chapter packs/quizzes serve this pair directly.

## What we reject

- Blinkist / Shortform-style **substitutes** that strip anecdotes, humor, stories, and self-relation.
- Regurgitated “teach the book” summaries; Shortform wall-of-text with weak recall.
- Dry fact dumps — *Sapiens* works because of voice and stories; that is the point.
- DIY “chat my PDF / chapter-by-chapter NotebookLM” as a peer product — inconsistent, bad UX for return-to-book companion (**why this exists**). Attention alternative only — not competitive peer.

## Who it is for (lean)

Built **for Brian first** — the gold standard is what he wants to use. Others may partake when that gold is worth his time to share. Commercial ICP follows dogfood, not the other way around.

## Guiding principle (company)

We want people to **read more** — that is why this site exists. We help them **understand** what they read, and we **encourage reflection** on what they have read. The **1 free companion every 3 months** (all tiers) is how that principle shows up in the offer — not a side promo. See [decisions/2026-07-22-guiding-principle-read-understand-reflect.md](decisions/2026-07-22-guiding-principle-read-understand-reflect.md).

## What we want

Tools that improve **recall of a book the user is actually consuming** — especially **return-to-book** confidence — plus **understanding** and **reflection**. Drive people **into** the source they already bought; increase finish rates and perceived value → publishers should like this; more books bought over time.
